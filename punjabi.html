Paneer Butter Masala

Paneer Butter Masala is a traditional Punjabi recipe in which soft pieces of Paneer are cooked in rich creamy butter & tomato gravy. It is one of the most popular dishes in Indian restaurants similar to Kadai Paneer, Palak Paneer, Shahi Paneer, Aloo Paneer, etc.

Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a popular Punjabi dish in India. The dish is a combination of chana masala (spicy white chickpeas) and bhatura/Puri, a fried bread made from maida. Chole bhature is often eaten as a breakfast dish, sometimes accompanied with lassi. It can also be street food or a complete meal and may be accompanied with onions, pickled carrots, green chutney or achaar.

Butter Chicken

Butter chicken or murgh makhani is a curry of chicken in a spiced tomato, butter and cream sauce. It originated in India as a curry. It is similar to chicken tikka masala, which uses a tomato paste. The gravy is prepared by heating and mixing butter, chicken broth, and cream. Spices like fenugreek, garam masala, and Kashmiri red chili powder are also used. Cashew paste can also be added, and will make the gravy thicker. Once the gravy is prepared, the cooked chicken is chopped and added to it. It is usually served with naan, roti, parathas or steamed rice.

Sarson Ka Saag

Sarson da saag is a popular vegetarian dish from the Punjab region of the Indian subcontinent made from mustard greens (sarson) and spices. It is often served with makki di roti. It can be topped with either butter (unprocessed white or processed yellow butter) or more traditionally with ghee (clarified butter). Sarson ka saag is traditionally eaten with makki di roti (literally "unraised cornbread"). Some spinach (called palak in Punjabi) may be added for added color and thickening the dish, even if this could alter the taste. Some people also add a little bit of mooli (white radish) to enhance the taste.

Amritsari Kulcha

Amritsari Kulcha is a type of stuffed bread (paratha) which is baked in a tandoor. It is a typical Punjabi recipe and is famous all over the world. It is made from maida flour, stuffed with boiled, mashed potatoes and lots of spices and chutney. It is served with chole (chickpeas) and is a popular breakfast in Punjab. It is also a popular street food in Delhi and Amritsar. It is also known as Amritsari naan.
