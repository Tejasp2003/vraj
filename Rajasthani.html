Daal Baati Churma

Daal baati churma is a popular rajasthani dish consisting of daal (lentils), baati (hard wheat rolls), and churma (crushed baati mixed with sugar or gur (jaggery) and ghee (clarified butter). It is usually served with rice and/or roti (bread). Baati is a hard, unleavened bread cooked in the desert areas of rajasthan. It is prized for its long shelf life and high nutritional content, and, in desert areas, for the minimal quantity of water required for its preparation. Baati is always eaten with dal (lentils) or with besan (gram flour) ki kadi (a curry of besan flour and yoghurt), with churma (a powdered sweet dish made of wheat, jaggery and ghee) and with a glass of buttermilk.
