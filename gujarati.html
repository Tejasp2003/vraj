Gujarati Menu

Undhiyu

Undhiyu is a Gujarati mixed vegetable dish that is a regional specialty of Surat, India. The name of this dish comes from the Gujarati words "matlu" meaning earthen pot and "undhu" meaning upside down since they have been traditionally cooked upside down underground in earthen pots fired from above.

£ 11

Dhokla

Dhokla is a vegetarian culinary dish that is found mainly in the Indian state of Gujarat and parts of adjacent states. It is made with a fermented batter derived from rice and split chickpeas. Dhokla can be eaten for breakfast, as a main course, as a side dish, or as a snack.

£ 17

Khandvi

Khandvi is a savory snack in Gujarati cuisine. It consists of yellowish, tightly rolled bite-sized pieces, and is primarily made of gram flour and yogurt. The batter is cooked into a thick paste, spread flat and then cut into bite-sized pieces.

Khichu

Khichu is a dough for making papad, however owing to its taste it is also consumed as Farsan. It is prepared by cooking flour in water with cumin seeds and salt. It is a popular snack in Gujarat.

£ 21

Sev Khamani

Sev khamani is a popular Gujarati snack. It is made from crumbled khaman dhokla tempered with mustard seeds, green chillies and topped with sev. It is served garnished with pomegranate and sev with fried chillies.

£ 15

Ghari

Ghari is a sweet dish from Gujarati cuisine, from the region of Surat. Ghari are made of puri batter, milk 'mawa', ghee and sugar - made into round shapes with sweet filling, to be consumed on Chandani Padva festival.

£ 11

Muthia

Muthia is a traditional Gujarati snack. It is made with chickpea flour, methi, salt, turmeric, chili powder, baking soda, sugar, oil, and water. The ingredients are mixed together to form a dough, rolled into sausage-like cylindrical shapes, steamed, and then pan-fried in oil with mustard seeds, sesame seeds, and sometimes curry leaves.

£ 14
